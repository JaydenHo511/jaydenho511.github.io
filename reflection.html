Reflection

Python turned out to share many similarities with Pyret, something which was unexpected for me.
Having similarities between languages made the first few lessons go by smoothly.
However, having minute differences made it hard, as I had some of these differences memorized based on Pyret instead of Python.

In the end, I spent so much time with little things that I ended up not giving myself enough time for Hangman, which I had planned to do as an artifact for this project.
I followed a tutorial which was rather fast-paced and didn't fully understand the concepts for the game.
Time constraints also played a part, as there were some parts of the code that I didn't understand due to not learning it in time.

Next time I take on a project like this, I need to work on my time management by knowing my priorities instead of following the lesson plans,
especially since I am on a strict schedule instead of an open schedule like the PY4E lesson were designed for.
I also feel that I may have been too ambitious when planning and doing to proposal for this project,
selecting something that requires a lot of work without considering my other classes and workload.
